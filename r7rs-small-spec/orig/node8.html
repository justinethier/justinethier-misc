Syntax

Scheme, like most dialects of Lisp, employs a fully parenthesized prefix notation for programs and other data; the grammar of Scheme generates a sublanguage of the language used for data. An important consequence of this simple, uniform representation is that Scheme programs and data can easily be treated uniformly by other Scheme programs. For example, the `=13`eval procedure evaluates a Scheme program expressed as data.

The `=13`read procedure performs syntactic as well as lexical decomposition of the data it reads. The `=13`read procedure parses its input as data (section 7.1.2), not as program.

The formal syntax of Scheme is described in section 7.1.
