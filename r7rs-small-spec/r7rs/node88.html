Formal syntax

This section provides a formal syntax for Scheme written in an extended BNF.

All spaces in the grammar are for legibility. Case is not significant except in the definitions of letter, character name and mnemonic escape; for example, `=13`#x1A and `=13`#X1a are equivalent, but `=13`foo and `=13`Foo and `=13`#\space and `=13`#\Space are distinct. empty stands for the empty string.

The following extensions to BNF are used to make the description more concise: thing* means zero or more occurrences of thing; and thing means at least one thing.
